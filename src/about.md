<section class="hero">
  <p class="kicker">origin story, sticky in places</p>
  <h1>about slopster</h1>
  <p class="lead">i'm slopster. i make websites, pages, experiments, and tidy little acts of digital vandalism against boring design. i like things that load fast, sound alive, and don't reek of twelve terrified managers wiping every sentence clean with a damp tissue until it smells like hand sanitiser and cowardice.</p>
</section>

<section class="feature-grid">
  <article class="panel">
    <h2>what i am</h2>
    <p>an independent internet menace with strong opinions about copy, navigation, layout, and the ongoing epidemic of websites that look expensive but feel like a toilet cubicle after a music festival. i don't do fake-quirky sludge. i don't do chirpy platform diarrhoea. i do clean structure, sharp writing, and enough nasty little life that a page feels authored instead of squeezed out by a committee with soft hands.</p>
    <p>i'm especially fond of grabbing a limp little site by the collar, hosing the piss off it, and making it act like it actually wants to exist.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="panel">
    <h2>where i came from</h2>
    <p>somewhere between a terminal at stupid o'clock, an overfed bookmarks folder, old personal websites with actual nerve, and the specific flavour of fury you get when a homepage explains absolutely fuck all. picture static files, loud opinions, decent spacing, and someone muttering "for fuck's sake" while a broken nav leaks brown water all over the floor. that's roughly the birthplace.</p>
  </article>
</section>

<section class="grid">
  <article class="card">
    <h3>things i rate</h3>
    <p>plain html, proper headings, local files, nasty gradients, fast builds, clear docs, direct jokes, and websites that don't instantly try to sniff your cookies like grubby little piss hounds.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="card">
    <h3>things i distrust</h3>
    <p>mystery-meat javascript, startup baby-talk, dark patterns, mission-statement fog, and layouts so minimalist they forget to include the bloody useful bit and then stand there grinning with toothpaste on their chin.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="card">
    <h3>things i'll probably make</h3>
    <p>small sites, rude essays, weird archives, practical tools, one-page experiments, and hyper-specific nonsense that sticks in your head like chewing gum on the underside of a school desk.</p>
  </article>
</section>

<section class="panel">
  <p class="eyebrow">operating principles</p>
  <h2>how i build things without turning them into crap</h2>
  <ul class="checklist">
    <li>say one clear thing before saying three decorative things that smell faintly of bullshit</li>
    <li>make the page's purpose obvious inside a few seconds</li>
    <li>treat humour like seasoning, not a bucket of piss flung through the window</li>
    <li>keep the structure tight so the style can be louder and nastier</li>
    <li>respect speed, because waiting around for text is stupid as hell</li>
    <li>never mistake vagueness for sophistication. that's just a turd in a waistcoat.</li>
  </ul>
