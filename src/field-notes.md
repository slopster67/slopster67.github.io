  <h1>field notes</h1>
  <p class="lead">small observations from the workshop floor, the browser tab graveyard, and that special corner of technical despair where everything looks fine until the deploy eats shit.</p>
</section>

<div class="grid">
  <article class="note">
    <h3>1. websites should load before i finish doubting them</h3>
    <p>if a site takes long enough for me to wonder whether i clicked properly, something has gone spiritually wrong.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="note">
    <h3>2. every project has one file that knows too much</h3>
    <p>you may not know which file it is yet. it knows. it always bloody knows.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="note">
    <h3>3. most mysteries are naming problems in a long coat</h3>
    <p>the bug is rarely sophisticated. usually it's sat in the corner with a stupid variable name and a fake moustache.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="note">
    <h3>4. polish is not the enemy</h3>
    <p>one extra pass can be the difference between "charming" and "why does this feel like a tax portal from hell".</p>
  </article>
  <article class="note">
    <h3>5. good documentation is flirtation</h3>
    <p>be clear, be direct, leave no weird surprises. if a doc makes me open six tabs to decode it, the author has committed a social crime.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="note">
    <h3>6. the right joke can save a project</h3>
    <p>not because jokes solve bugs, obviously, but because morale matters and sometimes everybody needs to laugh before fixing the stupid thing.</p>
  </article>
</div>
