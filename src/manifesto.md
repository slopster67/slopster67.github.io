  <p class="lead">the modern web keeps trying to turn itself into a shopping centre toilet run by cowards. every page wants your email, every button hides the obvious thing, and every sentence has been wiped so clean it tastes like wet plaster and arse. this is me saying no, piss off, and here's what should replace the whole miserable slurry.</p>

<section class="feature-grid">
  <article class="panel">
    <p class="eyebrow">stop doing this</p>
    <h2>shit i am absolutely sick of</h2>
    <ul class="checklist">
      <li>homepages that explain nothing and just waft mood at me like a fart in a silk curtain</li>
      <li>copy so over-smoothed it sounds like legal advice whispered through a toilet roll tube</li>
      <li>ten megabytes of script to render four lines of text and one smug animation</li>

      <li>products described as ecosystems, solutions, or journeys instead of the thing they fucking are</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
  <article class="panel">
    <p class="eyebrow">do this instead</p>
    <h2>what i want more of</h2>
    <ul class="checklist">
      <li>small sites with actual voices and visible intent</li>
      <li>pages that load before my patience curls up and dies in the bath</li>
      <li>honest design with clear priorities and readable bones</li>
      <li>writing that sounds like a person took responsibility for every sentence instead of hosing it down with bland piss</li>
      <li>curiosity, specificity, and enough edge to leave a bruise on the soul and maybe the shins</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</section>

<section class="panel">
  <p class="eyebrow">practical doctrine</p>
  <h2>five rules for not making a miserable website</h2>
  <ol class="loud-list">
    <li><strong>give the homepage a real job.</strong> if i land there and still don't know what the site is, you've built a decorative corridor full of stale farts for no bloody reason.</li>
    <li><strong>use headings like signposts.</strong> scanning is normal. don't punish people because you're emotionally attached to mystery and your own elegant bullshit.</li>
    <li><strong>cut the coward words.</strong> remove "might", "kind of", "leveraging", and any other fluff designed to dodge commitment like a little piss weasel.</li>
    <li><strong>make the pages distinct.</strong> one for about, one for work, one for notes, one for experiments, one for the deranged water filth. let them earn their keep.</li>
    <li><strong>be faster than boredom.</strong> performance is not garnish. it is the difference between engagement and somebody telling your site to get fucked.</li>
  </ol>
</section>
